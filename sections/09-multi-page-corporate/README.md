# 09 -- Multi-Page Corporate

## Design Philosophy

Traditional multi-page structure with clear hierarchy, professional tone, and familiar navigation patterns. This template rejects trendy single-page scrolling in favor of the proven multi-page layout that corporate audiences trust. Breadcrumbs, page-level navigation, and structured information architecture make content easy to find. Nothing surprising -- and that is the point.

## UX Pattern

The site has three distinct pages: Home, Services, and About.

**Home** opens with a split-right hero (headline left, image right) and flows through service highlights, credibility stats, client testimonials, and a clear CTA. It serves as a gateway, linking deeper into Services and About.

**Services** starts with a centered hero that includes a breadcrumb for orientation. Detailed service cards expand on the Home page previews, followed by a process-flow section (steps) explaining how engagements work, and a clean pricing comparison table.

**About** tells the company story, introduces the team via a headshot grid, includes deeper client testimonials, and ends with a contact section (form plus physical address).

Navigation uses a standard top bar with an active page indicator and a sliding underline animation. Sub-pages show breadcrumbs. Page transitions use a simple fade. Card hovers deepen shadows subtly. The contact form has inline validation states. No scroll-jacking, no parallax, no scroll-triggered animations -- just clean page-to-page browsing.

Thirteen sections total across three pages.

## When to Use

- Law firms, accounting practices, or consulting firms that need to look established
- Insurance companies and financial advisory services
- B2B enterprises with structured service catalogs
- Businesses migrating from older websites where users expect traditional navigation
- Any client who specifically asks for "separate pages" rather than a single long scroll

## When NOT to Use

- Startups or brands that want to feel modern, bold, or disruptive
- Creative agencies, design studios, or portfolio-driven businesses
- Products targeting younger demographics who expect single-page experiences
- Brands with minimal content -- three pages will feel hollow if there is not enough to fill them
- Clients who want scroll-driven storytelling or heavy animation -- this template has neither
