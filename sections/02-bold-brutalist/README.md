# 02 -- Bold Brutalist

## Design Philosophy

Raw, high-contrast, unapologetically loud. This template throws out conventional polish in favor of exposed structure, oversized typography, and asymmetric grids. It treats the web page like a concrete wall -- bold, unfinished, and impossible to ignore. Every design choice is deliberate provocation.

## UX Pattern

The user hits a split-hero with an enormous headline (clamped between 4rem and 8rem) and a hard-cropped image. There is no smooth scrolling -- sections cut jarringly from one to the next on purpose. The cursor changes to a crosshair over interactive elements. Hover states are aggressive: elements jitter via translateX, colors invert, and cards tilt with perspective transforms.

The headline uses a text-scramble animation on load. Navigation links get a thick underline that slides in from the left. The stats section runs counter animations on scroll. The final CTA is not a button -- it is the entire section, clickable as one giant block.

Seven sections in total: hero, asymmetric features, accent stats, single testimonial with oversized quotation marks, full-bleed media with overlaid text, left-aligned steps with thick underlines, and a full-section CTA.

## When to Use

- Creative agencies that want their site to feel like a statement piece
- Fashion or streetwear brands with an edgy, anti-corporate identity
- Music artists, labels, or event promoters
- Art galleries, exhibitions, or design studios
- Any brand that actively wants to alienate the "safe choice" crowd and attract people who value boldness

## When NOT to Use

- Corporate or enterprise clients who need to project trust and stability
- Healthcare, finance, or legal firms where readability and accessibility are paramount
- E-commerce sites where the product grid needs to be the focus
- Clients who are uncomfortable with unconventional layouts or "broken" design
- Projects targeting older demographics or audiences unfamiliar with brutalist web aesthetics
